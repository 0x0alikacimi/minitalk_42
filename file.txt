Choose a Research Problem: Pick a specific issue or question you want to study.

Review Existing Information: Read and study what others have already written about your topic.

Set Goals and Questions: Decide what you want to achieve with your research and what specific questions you will answer.

Plan Your Research: Choose how you will collect and analyze your data.

Select a Sample: Decide who or what you will study to represent the larger group you're interested in.

Collect Data: Gather information using your chosen methods.

Analyze Data: Look at the data to find patterns or answers to your questions.

Test Hypotheses: Check if your initial guesses (hypotheses) are supported by the data.

Draw Conclusions: Make sense of your findings and see how they fit with what you already know.

Write a Report: Summarize your research process, findings, and conclusions in a report.
